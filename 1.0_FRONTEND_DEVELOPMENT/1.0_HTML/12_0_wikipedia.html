Sir Timothy John Berners-Lee (born 8 June 1955), also known as TimBL, is an English computer scientist best known as the inventor of the World Wide Web, the HTML markup language, the URL system, and HTTP.

1. Early Life

Berners-Lee was born in London. His parents both worked on the Ferranti Mark 1, one of the first commercial computers. He studied at The Queen's College, Oxford, graduating with a first-class degree in physics.

2. Invention of the Web

While working at CERN in 1989, Berners-Lee proposed an information management system using hypertext. By 1991 the first website was live. He made the Web royalty-free so it could grow freely across the world.

3. Later Career

Berners-Lee founded the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) at MIT in 1994 to develop open web standards. He continues to advocate for an open and decentralised web for all people.

4. Awards & Honours

He received a knighthood (KBE) in 2004, the Turing Award in 2016, and was inducted into the Internet Hall of Fame in 2012.
